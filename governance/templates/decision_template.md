## Context

What situation or question prompted this decision. What already existed, what was missing or in tension, what triggered looking at it now.

## Decision

The one-paragraph statement of what was decided. Precise enough that a future reader knows exactly what changed and what didn't.

## Rationale

Why this decision, not just what it is. Cite the evidence (backtest, data, prior decision) it rests on.

## Alternatives Considered

What else was on the table and why it lost. A decision record without this section usually means the alternatives weren't seriously considered — if there genuinely were none, say so explicitly rather than omitting the section.

## Consequences

What changes going forward as a direct result of this decision — and, just as importantly, what does *not* change. Name anything explicitly out of scope so a future reader doesn't assume this decision authorized more than it did.
