## Business summary

GE Vernova supplies power-generation and electrification equipment
(gas turbines, grid/electrification products) into a demand
environment that includes, but is not limited to, AI data-center power
buildout. Fiscal year 2025: orders $59.3 billion (+34% organic), total
backlog $150 billion (+$31.2 billion year-over-year), revenue $38.1
billion, net income $4.9 billion (including a $2.9 billion tax
benefit). Q1 2026: the Electrification segment booked $2.4 billion in
equipment orders supporting data centers alone -- more than all of
2025 combined -- and total company backlog grew more than $13 billion
quarter-over-quarter, to $163 billion.

## Investment thesis (business-quality description, not a
recommendation)

**Concise statement:** GE Vernova is a large-scale power and
electrification equipment supplier with reported, growing order and
backlog exposure to AI data-center power demand, operating in a
business whose execution risk centers on large, long-cycle,
fixed/competitively-bid contracts.

**Expanded narrative:** the Electrification segment's reported Q1 2026
data-center order figure ($2.4 billion in a single quarter, exceeding
all of 2025) is a concrete, dated, sourced growth signal distinct from
the company's broader backlog growth. The FY2025 total backlog figure
($150 billion, +$31.2 billion year-over-year) and Q1 2026 sequential
growth (+$13 billion, to $163 billion) describe a company converting
demand into booked orders at scale -- though backlog is not revenue,
and conversion pace carries its own disclosed execution risk (see
Risks below).

**Conditions relevant to monitoring this thesis** (qualitative,
observable -- no arbitrary numerical threshold, consistent with
PI-0004's precedent for COST/XOM):
- Electrification segment data-center order flow continues at or
  above recently reported levels, without a material, sustained
  reversal
- Total backlog continues growing or is converted to revenue at a
  pace consistent with recent disclosure
- No material, disclosed deterioration in Wind or European
  Electrification margins beyond what is already flagged
- Progress toward management's stated ~$200 billion total-backlog
  target by year-end 2028 continues to be disclosed on pace

**Conditions that would weaken confidence in the above** (analytical
judgment, qualitative and observable, no numerical trigger):
- A material, sustained decline in Electrification segment
  data-center order flow
- Evidence of material backlog-to-revenue conversion failure (orders
  booked but not converting to delivered revenue on a reasonable
  timeline)
- A material, disclosed supply-chain-driven project delay
- A material further deterioration in Wind or European Electrification
  margins

## Conviction

**Rating: Medium**

**Rationale:** GE Vernova's FY2025 orders ($59.3B, +34% organic), total
backlog ($150B, +$31.2B YoY), and Q1 2026 data-center-specific
Electrification orders ($2.4B in a single quarter) reflect real,
demonstrated demand tied to the AI-infrastructure power buildout.
Conviction is Medium rather than High because the company's own
disclosed risk profile centers on execution across large, long-cycle,
fixed/competitively-bid contracts, with margin softness specifically
flagged in Wind and European Electrification. This rating explicitly
excludes valuation, entry-price, allocation, trading, and margin
judgments.
