## Business summary

Thermo Fisher Scientific supplies life-sciences research tools,
analytical instruments, diagnostics, and laboratory products and
biopharma services across four disclosed segments, with a large
recurring consumables, reagents, and services revenue base
concentrated in the Laboratory Products and Biopharma Services
segment. For fiscal year 2025: total revenue was $44.56 billion, up
4%. Reported segment revenue: Life Sciences Solutions $10.37 billion
(+8%); Analytical Instruments $7.55 billion (+1%); Specialty
Diagnostics $4.68 billion (+4%); Laboratory Products and Biopharma
Services $23.98 billion (+4%). **Laboratory Products and Biopharma
Services is the largest reported segment by segment revenue.** The
four segment totals sum to approximately $46.58 billion, which is
**not directly additive** to the $44.56 billion consolidated total --
the approximately $2.0 billion difference was not reconciled against a
directly inspected eliminations table, and **no conclusion in this
record relies on summing the four segment figures.**

## Investment thesis (business-quality description, not a
recommendation)

**Concise statement:** Thermo Fisher is a diversified life-sciences
tools and services company with positive reported growth across all
four disclosed segments in FY2025, anchored by a large recurring-
revenue base in its largest segment, Laboratory Products and Biopharma
Services.

**Expanded narrative:** the thesis rests on continued, or eventually
recovering, biopharma and laboratory end-market demand. Growth is real
but muted in places -- Analytical Instruments grew only 1% for the
full year, reflecting disclosed softness in part of the lab and
biopharma end market, distinct from the stronger growth reported in
Life Sciences Solutions (+8%). This is reported here as the current
disclosed condition, distinct from any recovery expectation TMO's own
forward guidance may separately express, and is not itself a
prediction of when or whether such a recovery occurs.

**Conditions relevant to monitoring this thesis** (qualitative,
observable -- no arbitrary numerical threshold, consistent with
PI-0004's precedent for COST/XOM):
- Laboratory Products and Biopharma Services segment revenue continues
  growing at or above recently reported rates
- Analytical Instruments segment growth improves from its currently
  muted level, or at minimum does not deteriorate further
- No material, sustained reversal in Life Sciences Solutions or
  Specialty Diagnostics segment growth

**Conditions that would weaken confidence in the above** (analytical
judgment, qualitative and observable, no numerical trigger):
- A material, sustained deceleration or reversal in Laboratory
  Products and Biopharma Services segment revenue
- A material, further deterioration in Analytical Instruments segment
  growth
- Evidence of a broader, disclosed biopharma/laboratory end-market
  downturn beyond what is currently reported

## Conviction

**Rating: Medium**

**Rationale:** TMO's FY2025 results show positive reported growth in
all four disclosed segments -- Life Sciences Solutions up 8% to $10.37
billion, Analytical Instruments up 1% to $7.55 billion, Specialty
Diagnostics up 4% to $4.68 billion, and Laboratory Products and
Biopharma Services up 4% to $23.98 billion, the largest reported
segment by segment revenue. Growth is real but muted in places --
Analytical Instruments' 1% growth reflects disclosed softness in part
of the lab and biopharma end market. Conviction is Medium rather than
High specifically because of this currently muted growth in at least
one major segment, and because TMO's own enumerated Form 10-K risk-
factor text was not independently inspected in this research pass.
This rating explicitly excludes valuation, entry-price, allocation,
trading, and margin judgments, and was not inferred from TMO's
existing T2 portfolio-tier placement -- it was reasoned solely from
the segment-level growth pattern described above.
