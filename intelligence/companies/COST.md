## Business summary

Costco is a membership-based warehouse club operator. As of the June
2026 sales release (2026-07-08), Costco currently operates 933
warehouses [bundle W-01]. Fiscal year 2025 (ended 2025-08-31): 914
warehouses across the U.S./Puerto Rico (629) plus 12 other countries
(Canada 110, Mexico 42, Japan 37, U.K. 29, Korea 20, Australia 15,
Taiwan 14, China 7, Spain 5, France 2, Sweden 2, Iceland 1). Net sales
$269.912B (+8.1% YoY) [bundle F-01]. Net income $8.099B (+10% YoY).
Membership fee revenue $5.323B (+10.0% YoY) [bundle F-02]. FY2025
year-end renewal rates: 92.3% U.S./Canada, 89.8% worldwide [bundle
F-03]; 81.0M paid members [bundle F-04].

Third quarter fiscal 2026 (12 weeks ended 2026-05-10, reported
2026-05-28): net sales $69.154B (+11.6% YoY) [bundle F-05]; 36-week
year-to-date net sales $203.37B (+9.6%); net income $2.192B / $4.93 per
diluted share (vs. $1.90B / $4.28 prior year) [bundle F-14]; membership
fee income $1.373B (+10.7%) [bundle F-06]; 82.9M total paid memberships
(+4.1%) [bundle F-07]; 148.5M total cardholders (+4.0%) [bundle F-16];
41.2M Executive memberships, with 75.0% penetration of sales to
Executive members (company-defined measure) [bundle F-15];
U.S./Canada renewal rate 92.2%; worldwide renewal rate 89.7% [bundle
F-08]. Q3 FY2026 gross margin was 11.04%, down 21 basis points year
over year; excluding gasoline it was up 1 basis point, and core-on-core
sales margin was down 9 basis points (management-defined decomposition)
[bundle F-13].

Cash flow, first 36 weeks of fiscal 2026: net cash provided by
operating activities $11.133B [bundle F-09]; additions to property and
equipment $4.228B [bundle F-10]. Operating cash flow less additions to
property and equipment gives a $6.905B free-cash-flow proxy — **a
non-GAAP committee calculation on those two company-reported inputs,
not a company-reported metric** [bundle F-11]. No FY2026 full-year
free-cash-flow figure is asserted in this record (see Unresolved
items).

Revenue model is dual-track: low-margin bulk goods sold at scale,
funded structurally by recurring membership fees ($65/yr standard,
$130/yr Executive in the U.S., varying internationally).

## Investment thesis

**Concise statement:** Costco's membership-fee-funded low-margin model
produces unusually loyal, recurring revenue with a durable cost
advantage, evidenced by renewal rates holding near 90% worldwide, across
a still-expanding warehouse and digital footprint.

**Expanded narrative:** the core mechanism — sell near cost, monetize
through membership loyalty rather than markup — is difficult for
non-membership retailers to replicate without either a comparable fee
revenue stream or materially thinner margins. Renewal rates have stayed
above 89% worldwide across recent reporting periods. Growth optionality
comes from continued warehouse expansion (~26 net new warehouses guided
for fiscal 2026, including continued international expansion in China
and Mexico) and Costco's ongoing digital/e-commerce investment — the
digital-growth figures previously excluded pending period confirmation
are now confirmed and recorded above ("Digital and monthly sales
evidence").

**Conditions required for the thesis to remain valid** (qualitative,
observable — no arbitrary numerical threshold):
- Membership renewal behavior remains stable to improving, without a
  sustained multi-period decline
- Membership fee revenue growth keeps pace with the underlying business
- Warehouse expansion continues at a pace consistent with recent
  guidance, without a material reduction
- Labor-cost pressure is offset by scale, pricing, and membership
  economics rather than persistently eroding operating performance

**Thesis-weakening conditions** (analytical judgment, approved as part
of this record — observable and qualitative, deliberately without a
specific numerical trigger):
- Sustained deterioration in membership renewal behavior
- Membership-fee growth persistently lagging the underlying business
- Evidence that digital member acquisition materially weakens member
  quality, beyond the mild effect already observed in recent renewal
  data
- Persistent margin pressure that scale, pricing, or membership
  economics do not offset
- Material loss of Costco's relative purchasing or member-value
  advantage versus competitors

**Factual status of the observables (as of 2026-07-23, facts only — no
trigger is redefined and no thesis conclusion is drawn):** renewal
rates moved from 92.3%/89.8% at FY2025 year-end to 92.2%/89.7% at Q3
FY2026 — a -0.1 percentage-point change in each measure across
different reporting dates, which alone does not establish sustained
deterioration (committee arithmetic) [bundle R-02]. The observable
evidence through fiscal Q3 2026 showed no membership-fee contraction
(Q3 +10.7%) and no digitally-enabled quarter below 20%, while the
21-basis-point gross-margin decline and the 1.0-point May-to-June
adjusted-comp slowdown are observable adverse facts (committee status
synthesis) [bundle R-03].

## Competitive advantages — detail

*(YAML holds concise claims only, per the frozen schema; fuller
evidence and counterarguments live here.)*

1. **Membership-fee-funded low-margin model.** Evidence: $5,323M FY2025
   membership fee revenue funding sub-market goods pricing [bundle
   F-02]. Counterargument: Walmart's Sam's Club runs a structurally
   similar model at smaller scale — this advantage is relative, not
   unique.
2. **High renewal rates (92.2%/89.7%).** Evidence: Q3 FY2026 disclosure
   [bundle F-08]; 92.3%/89.8% at FY2025 year-end [bundle F-03].
   Counterargument: renewal rate has ticked down slightly as digital
   member acquisition grows — a trend worth monitoring (see the
   thesis-status note above) [bundle R-02].
3. **Bulk purchasing scale.** Evidence: 933 currently operated
   warehouses per the June 2026 release [bundle W-01] (914 at FY2025
   year-end), $269.912B FY2025 net sales [bundle F-01]. Counterargument:
   Walmart's overall purchasing scale is larger, though its
   warehouse-club format (Sam's Club) is smaller than Costco's.
4. **Concentrated SKU assortment.** Evidence: a long-documented Costco
   operating characteristic supporting inventory turnover.
   Counterargument: the same trait limits selection breadth, a real
   (if usually minor) member friction point.

**Kirkland Signature / private-label evidence** (supporting advantages
1 and 4; qualitative only): Costco states that Kirkland Signature
products are designed to be high quality and generally sold at prices
lower than national brands [bundle K-01], that they generally produce
higher margins than national brands, and that their sales penetration
continued to grow in fiscal 2025 [bundle K-02] — management
characterizations from the FY2025 10-K, not independent price or margin
studies. The Q3 FY2026 supplemental showed price reductions on four
named Kirkland items (selected management examples, not evidence of
portfolio-wide price changes) [bundle K-03]. **A widely-repeated
quantitative estimate of Kirkland's aggregate sales (~$90B, roughly a
quarter to a third of merchandise sales) could not be confirmed in the
inspected 10-K or Q3 supplemental and is deliberately excluded from
this record** [bundle K-04].

  bargaining is a **union allegation** — no NLRB adjudication or

- 2025-01-20 — the Teamsters announced an 85% strike-authorization

- 2025-02-01 — a new national agreement was entered effective this

### *Stockov v. Costco* tariff-refund litigation

*Allegations are never established conduct; the distinction is
preserved in every statement below.*

- **Filing:** *Stockov v. Costco Wholesale Corporation*, N.D. Ill.
  No. 1:26-cv-02734, filed 2026-03-11 [bundle T-01].
- **Proposed class (a pleading, not a certified class):** nationwide
  purchasers of Costco goods subject to IEEPA tariffs from 2025-02-01
  through 2026-02-24 [bundle T-02].
- **Allegations:** that Costco passed tariff costs to customers through
  higher prices and could obtain a "double recovery" if it retained
  government tariff refunds [bundle T-03].
- **Requested remedies (requested, not awarded):** restitution or
  damages, declaratory relief, interest, fees, and class treatment
  [bundle T-04].
- **Procedural facts:** Costco filed a motion to dismiss 2026-05-18;
  the public docket index set 2026-06-16 for the plaintiff's response
  and 2026-06-30 for Costco's reply (secondary-reported: Reuters,
  corroborated by the docket index) [bundle T-05]. In support of
  dismissal Costco stated it had not received the tariff refunds at
  issue and characterized the claims as speculative or premature — a
  management/legal position reported from a filing not directly
  rendered, not a court finding [bundle T-06].
- **Unresolved:** the complete procedural status after the 2026-06-30
  reply deadline — including whether the motion was decided or any
  class-certification motion was filed — could not be confirmed (full
  docket access-blocked, HTTP 403) [bundle T-07]. The reported
  statement that Costco's CEO would return any tariff-refund value to

## Catalyst

Fiscal 2026 warehouse expansion: approximately 26 net new warehouse
openings, per management guidance disclosed on the Q3 FY2026 earnings
call, including continued international expansion in China and Mexico.
Management's Q3 FY2026 supplemental projects 940 warehouses at
fiscal-2026 year-end versus 914 at fiscal-2025 year-end — a
forward-looking management estimate (7 opened in Q1, 3 in Q2, 4 in Q3,
12 estimated for the remainder), consistent with the ~26-net-additions
guidance [bundle W-02]; 933 were operated as of the 2026-07-08 June
sales release [bundle W-01]. Expected: fiscal year-end 2026
(2026-08-31), genuinely tied to this catalyst since that is the
guidance period itself.

No other catalyst is included in this record. A previously considered
speculative membership-fee-increase catalyst was excluded because it
was not an announced company action. The e-commerce-growth figures
previously excluded pending period confirmation are now confirmed and
recorded as evidence (see "Digital and monthly sales evidence"), not as
a catalyst.
