## Business summary

Costco is a membership-based warehouse club operator. Fiscal year 2025
(ended 2025-08-31): 914 warehouses across the U.S./Puerto Rico (629)
plus 12 other countries (Canada 110, Mexico 42, Japan 37, U.K. 29,
Korea 20, Australia 15, Taiwan 14, China 7, Spain 5, France 2, Sweden 2,
Iceland 1). Net sales $269.9B (+8.1% YoY). Net income $8.099B (+10%
YoY). Membership fee revenue $5,323M (+10% YoY).

Third quarter fiscal 2026 (12 weeks ended 2026-05-10, reported
2026-05-28): net sales $69.15B (+11.6% YoY); 36-week year-to-date net
sales $203.37B (+9.6%); net income $2.19B / $4.93 per diluted share
(vs. $1.90B / $4.28 prior year); membership fee income $1.37B (+10.7%);
82.9M total paid members (+4.1%); 149M total cardholders (+4%);
U.S./Canada renewal rate 92.2% (+10 basis points sequentially);
worldwide renewal rate 89.7%.

Revenue model is dual-track: low-margin bulk goods sold at scale,
funded structurally by recurring membership fees ($65/yr standard,
$130/yr Executive in the U.S., varying internationally).

## Investment thesis

**Concise statement:** Costco's membership-fee-funded low-margin model
produces unusually loyal, recurring revenue with a durable cost
advantage, evidenced by renewal rates holding near 90% worldwide, across
a still-expanding warehouse and digital footprint.

**Expanded narrative:** the core mechanism — sell near cost, monetize
through membership loyalty rather than markup — is difficult for
non-membership retailers to replicate without either a comparable fee
revenue stream or materially thinner margins. Renewal rates have stayed
above 89% worldwide across recent reporting periods. Growth optionality
comes from continued warehouse expansion (~26 net new warehouses guided
for fiscal 2026, including continued international expansion in China
and Mexico) and Costco's ongoing digital/e-commerce investment, though
this record does not include specific recent digital-growth figures
pending confirmation of their exact reporting period.

**Conditions required for the thesis to remain valid** (qualitative,
observable — no arbitrary numerical threshold):
- Membership renewal behavior remains stable to improving, without a
  sustained multi-period decline
- Membership fee revenue growth keeps pace with the underlying business
- Warehouse expansion continues at a pace consistent with recent
  guidance, without a material reduction
- Labor-cost pressure is offset by scale, pricing, and membership
  economics rather than persistently eroding operating performance

**Thesis-weakening conditions** (analytical judgment, approved as part
of this record — observable and qualitative, deliberately without a
specific numerical trigger):
- Sustained deterioration in membership renewal behavior
- Membership-fee growth persistently lagging the underlying business
- Evidence that digital member acquisition materially weakens member
  quality, beyond the mild effect already observed in recent renewal
  data
- Persistent margin pressure that scale, pricing, or membership
  economics do not offset
- Material loss of Costco's relative purchasing or member-value
  advantage versus competitors

## Competitive advantages — detail

*(YAML holds concise claims only, per the frozen schema; fuller
evidence and counterarguments live here.)*

1. **Membership-fee-funded low-margin model.** Evidence: $5,323M FY2025
   membership fee revenue funding sub-market goods pricing.
   Counterargument: Walmart's Sam's Club runs a structurally similar
   model at smaller scale — this advantage is relative, not unique.
2. **High renewal rates (92.2%/89.7%).** Evidence: Q3 FY2026 disclosure.
   Counterargument: renewal rate has ticked down slightly as digital
   member acquisition grows — a trend worth monitoring.
3. **Bulk purchasing scale.** Evidence: 914 warehouses, $269.9B FY2025
   net sales. Counterargument: Walmart's overall purchasing scale is
   larger, though its warehouse-club format (Sam's Club) is smaller
   than Costco's.
4. **Concentrated SKU assortment.** Evidence: a long-documented Costco
   operating characteristic supporting inventory turnover.
   Counterargument: the same trait limits selection breadth, a real
   (if usually minor) member friction point.

## Risks — detail

Category framing (not a schema field, recorded here only):
- Competition: structural/cyclical — competitive intensity is
  persistent, not a one-time event
- Labor costs: structural/operational — driven by a multi-year employee
  compensation agreement; this record does not include the exact wage
  figures, as they could not be independently verified from a primary
  source within the disclosed evidence standard
- Renewal-rate digital-mix drag: operational, currently minor
- E-commerce execution: operational
- Tariff/FX exposure: structural, partially hedged via forward
  foreign-exchange contracts

Observable indicators worth monitoring: quarterly renewal-rate trend
(U.S./Canada and worldwide), membership fee revenue growth rate versus
net sales growth rate, and any material change in wage or labor-cost
policy.

## Catalyst

Fiscal 2026 warehouse expansion: approximately 26 net new warehouse
openings, per management guidance disclosed on the Q3 FY2026 earnings
call, including continued international expansion in China and Mexico.
Expected: fiscal year-end 2026 (2026-08-31), genuinely tied to this
catalyst since that is the guidance period itself.

No other catalyst is included in this record. A previously considered
speculative membership-fee-increase catalyst and an e-commerce-growth
catalyst were both excluded — the former because it was not an
announced company action, the latter because its exact reporting
period, figures, and disclosure date could not be confirmed at the
disclosed evidence standard.

  development.

## Conviction

**Rating: High**

**Rationale:** Costco's membership model, renewal behavior, purchasing
scale, and operating consistency support high conviction in the
durability of the business-quality thesis. The company still faces
meaningful competitive, labor-cost, digital-execution, international,
and merchandise-cost risks. This rating explicitly excludes valuation,
entry-price, allocation, trading, and margin judgments.

This rating and rationale reflect human judgment, approved after
independent review of AI-assisted research — the research and drafting
process does not itself constitute the human judgment this record
requires; the approval decision does.
